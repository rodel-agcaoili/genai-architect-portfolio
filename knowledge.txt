OWASP Top 10 for Large Language Models (LLMs):
1. Prompt Injections: Manipulating the LLM via crafted inputs.
2. Insecure Output Handling: Failing to validate LLM outputs before downstream use.
3. Training Data Poisoning: Tampering with the model's training data.
4. Model Denial of Service: Overloading the LLM with resource-heavy requests.
5. Supply Chain Vulnerabilities: Compromised third-party components or models.
6. Sensitive Information Disclosure: LLMs leaking PII or confidential data.
7. Insecure Plugin Design: Exploiting weak access controls in LLM plugins.
8. Excessive Agency: Giving LLMs too much autonomy without human-in-the-loop.
9. Overreliance: Blindly trusting LLM outputs leading to misconfigurations.
10. Model Theft: Unauthorized access or exfiltration of proprietary models.
